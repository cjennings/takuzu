TAKUZU — BOARD PLATE PNG REQUIREMENTS
=====================================
For generating the gestell theme's board images (the "board plate" the
pieces drop into). One plate per board size. Drop finished files in this
inbox/ directory.

Reference: board.png (the lighter of the two you sent) is the approved
look and becomes gestell-board-8.png. Match the other four to it.


THE SET (five files)
--------------------
One plate per takuzu board size. The only thing that changes between them
is the number of wells per side:

  gestell-board-4.png    4 x 4  wells  (16 cells)
  gestell-board-6.png    6 x 6  wells  (36 cells)
  gestell-board-8.png    8 x 8  wells  (64 cells)   <- board.png, already done
  gestell-board-10.png  10 x 10 wells  (100 cells)
  gestell-board-12.png  12 x 12 wells  (144 cells)


PNG FORMAT
----------
- Shape: square (1:1). The board is square at every size.
- Size: high-res, square. What you've been getting (~1254 x 1254) is
  perfect. Use the SAME pixel dimensions for all five.
- Color: sRGB.
- Opacity: OPAQUE. No transparency, no alpha channel needed. The dark
  bronze frame sits mounted on the dark console and the corners blend in.
  Do NOT try to make the background transparent — it isn't needed here,
  and a flat opaque plate is exactly right. (Transparency only matters for
  the PIECES, which are handled separately.)


STYLE — keep the five a matched family
--------------------------------------
Same prompt each time; change only the well count. Every plate must share:
- The same bronze / dark-metal material and patina.
- The same beveled outer frame, same thickness proportion.
- The same four corner screws.
- The same recessed-well style (rounded-square wells with bronze edges).

Aim for a similar frame width and a similar central grid area on every
plate. Don't let the 4x4 end up with a giant frame and four huge wells
while the 12x12 goes edge-to-edge. They should look like the same physical
plate re-machined with a finer or coarser grid.


THE ONE HARD REQUIREMENT — a regular grid
-----------------------------------------
The well grid must be REGULAR:
- Evenly spaced wells, uniform pitch (equal gap between every well).
- Symmetric margins on all four sides (grid centered on the plate).
- Square cells (wells as tall as they are wide).
- No warping, drift, or perspective — a flat, straight-on grid.

This is what lets the engine drop each piece dead-center in its well by a
simple formula. You do NOT need to hit exact pixel positions — I measure
each plate's grid off the image and calibrate placement (that's how the
8x8 lined up on the first try). I only need the grid itself to be even and
straight.

For reference, board.png measured out to: first well center ~14.5% in from
the edge, wells spaced ~10% of the plate width apart, well opening a bit
under one pitch wide. Your other sizes don't have to match those numbers —
just be internally regular and symmetric.


WATCH THE HIGH SIZES
--------------------
AI grids tend to drift at higher counts. A 6x6 usually comes out clean; a
12x12 is more likely to come out slightly uneven. Before sending, eyeball
the 10x10 and 12x12 for even spacing and straight rows/columns. If one
comes out warped, send it anyway and flag it — I can measure each well
individually as a fallback, or you regenerate.


NAMING + DROP
-------------

- Drop them in this inbox/ directory.


WHAT YOU DON'T HAVE TO WORRY ABOUT
----------------------------------
- Transparency: none needed on the plates (see above).
- Exact piece placement: I measure and calibrate per plate.
- Piece sprites: the taijitu and gear are handled separately by the
  masking pipeline; you don't put pieces on the plate. The plate is just
  the empty board.
- Resizing to game scale: the engine scales each plate to fit; give me the
  high-res master.


WHEN THEY LAND
--------------
I measure each plate's grid, calibrate piece placement, and render all five
in the full console so you can see the whole set at every size before we
wire it into the game for real.

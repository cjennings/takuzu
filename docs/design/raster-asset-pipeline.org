#+TITLE: Raster Asset Pipeline — Piece Art for Themes
#+AUTHOR: Craig Jennings
#+DATE: 2026-07-15

Practical guide for preparing raster piece art (cameo / photographic coins) for
the raster-sprite-per-state path. The design rationale and the render
requirement live in the spec: =docs/specs/2026-07-14-piece-theme-framework-spec.org=,
subsection "Raster assets and the render requirement." This file is the
asset-maker's checklist.

Proven 2026-07-15 in both renderers: Emacs svg.el/librsvg (live in-game) and the
browser (Blink).

* Source resolution

- Shape: square, 1:1 (the board cell is square).
- Size: 256 x 256 px per image.
  - The largest board cell is 50px in logical units, but the board auto-scales
    to fill the window (commonly 2-4x) and HiDPI doubles again, so a coin can
    land on screen around 200-400px. Render at 256 so it DOWNSCALES for smaller
    boards (always clean) and never has to UPSCALE (what blurs).
  - 200 x 200 is the practical floor.
  - Do not exceed ~512.
  - KEEP 256 even for performance. The engine embeds each unique sprite ONCE and
    references it per cell (define-once + =<use>=), so sprite size barely affects
    render cost. Do NOT drop to 100px to speed things up: that lowers quality and
    fixes nothing. Measured 2026-07-15, a naive per-cell inline of 256px wood
    coins gave a 4.2 MB board SVG; define-once dropped it to 260 KB, flat with
    coin count, at full resolution.
- Format: PNG-24 with alpha (RGBA), sRGB.

* How many images (one per distinct piece state)

- Minimum 2: one per piece value (value 0, value 1). Mark "given" cells with an
  overlay drawn in code (the proof used a gold ring), so you still render only 2
  source images.
- Up to 4 if you want givens to be their own art: value-0-player, value-1-player,
  value-0-given, value-1-given.

Suggested names: =cameo-0.png=, =cameo-1.png= (add =-given= variants if using 4).

* Transparency

- Reality first: the exporters used so far flatten transparency on export (to
  solid white, once to a baked checkerboard), even when the art was made
  transparent. So in practice you ALWAYS mask the coin to its shape and do not
  rely on getting real alpha out of the generator. The mask is
  background-agnostic (white, checkerboard, or transparent all give the same
  clean round sprite), so chasing real source transparency buys nothing but
  skipping the mask. Mask everything.
- Prefer transparent (the alpha channel) WHEN you can get it. A transparent
  background lets the theme's substrate and glow show around the coin, so it sits
  in the lit world instead of as a tile. This matters most on a light or mid-tone
  theme background, where an opaque corner reads as a square tile.
- Do NOT color-key the background to transparent. Flat-color keying bites into
  the shadowed parts of the image. Mask by SHAPE, not by color.
- Shape-masking is for round coins. A non-round symbol (a star, a gear
  silhouette) needs masking to its own outline, or art authored with real alpha:
  a per-symbol call.

* Circular-mask export (round coins)

Adjust the radius to sit just inside the coin's edge (drop the corners, keep the
bevel). For a disc that fills a 1254px frame, radius ~617 works:

#+begin_src sh
magick src.png \
  \( -size 1254x1254 xc:none -fill white -draw "circle 627,627 627,10" \) \
  -alpha off -compose CopyOpacity -composite \
  -resize 256x256 out.png
#+end_src

(=circle 627,627 627,10= = centre 627,627, radius 617; =CopyOpacity= keeps the
image only where the white circle is opaque, so the corners become transparent.)

* Lighting

Bake the piece's lighting into the sprite to match the board's key light from the
top-left (or, for a glow theme, its glow), so the coin reads as seated rather than
pasted. A sprite lit from the wrong direction looks stuck on. The wood test's
beveled rim, lit top-left, is why it sits convincingly.

* Small-size legibility

At 12x12 the coin is about 26px, and fine texture (grain, fine detail) washes out
there. That is fine: a good raster piece is carried by tone and baked relief (the
light/dark split, the bevel), not fine texture. Judge a raster theme at 12x12, not
just at 6x6.

* The render requirement (engine side, not asset side)

Repeated here because it decides whether the art performs: a raster drawer must
embed each unique image ONCE in a =<defs>= and reference it per cell with =<use>=,
never inline the image per cell. See the spec subsection for the measured numbers
and the =takuzu--ensure-coin-gradient= idiom it follows.

* Net

Give 256x256 RGBA PNGs, one per value (2 total, or 4 if givens get their own art),
circle-masked to transparent. Keep 256; the engine's define-once render makes the
size nearly free.
